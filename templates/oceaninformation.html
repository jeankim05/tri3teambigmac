Oceans

What are Oceans?

Oceans are huge bodies of saltwater that cover a total of about 70% of Earth's surface. Although there is technically one ocean in the world, they are divided into four distinct sections: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, and Arctic. The Pacific Ocean is considered the largest ocean and is home to about 50% of the United States that is underwater and close to 25,000 islands.

Oceans are important as they produce over 70% of our planet's oxygen levels. Oceans average 12,100 feet in depth and are home to great tourist destinations or natural monuments. They also house a vast variety of wildlife such as blue whales, the largest current organism. Some even measure up to 108 feet!
